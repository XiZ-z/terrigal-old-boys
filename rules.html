Scoring rules

Straight off the weekly score sheet.

Points per set

  • 1 point for each set won
  • 0.5 points each for a drawn set (4–4)
  • 6 sets are played each week, so up to 6 points come from sets alone

Bonus points

  • 2 bonus points to the team that wins the most sets
  • 2 bonus points to the team that wins the most games
  • If tied on sets or games, 1 point each instead
  • Maximum 10 points available to a team each week (6 from sets + 4 bonus)

Match format

  • Round robin nights: 3 players per team, rotating pairs (1&2, 1&3, 2&3)
  • Finals (from the elimination round onward): all 4 players on the team play, not just 3
  • Grade pairs face the same grade pairing on the other team (e.g. 1&2 v 1&2)
  • Each grade pairing is played twice — 6 sets total across the night
  • Each set is played to 8 games — not first-to-6
  • All 4 players on court serve exactly twice, so every set totals 8 games
  • A 4–4 scoreline is a genuine draw, not just a rare tiebreak case

Season format

  • 8 teams, 4 courts, double round robin (14 rounds)
  • 2 reserve weeks for wet weather makeups
  • Top 6 make the finals series

Tiebreakers

  • Ladder ties: broken by overall set differential across the whole season, then overall game differential, then head-to-head (points, then sets, then games, from just the matches between the two tied teams) if still needed
  • Finals ties: decided by a tiebreaker set played to 7, no need to win by 2
  • Which pair on each team plays the finals tiebreaker is decided before the match

Governance

  • The committee is made up of the team captains
  • The committee selects the teams for each comp
  • Any changes to the competition need to be voted on by everyone

Costs

  • $15 court fees, paid each week
  • $25 registration, paid once per comp season
